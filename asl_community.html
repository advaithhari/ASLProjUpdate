American Sign Language is more than just a form of communication, it is a way for the Deaf community to connect with the outer world and experience the happy moments that life throws their way. Although these simple symbols may seem meaningless to us, they are what forge a bond that unite people suffering from hearing loss or even deafness. The members of this community not only share a common language, but are also linked by similar beliefs and attitudes which help them understand one another to a greater extent.
Currently, 1.5 billion people around the world live with some degree of hearing loss, and this number is projected to hit 2.5 billion by the end of 2050.If you want to be involved in the Deaf Community, you first need to start learning American Sign Language. ASL is the core bond within the Deaf community and Deaf Culture and is the first step for hearing people to get involved. At ASL, we offer a translator which can decrypt hand symbols and translate it to text. We hope you make an effort to reach out and support the Deaf community via our simplied and easy-to-use application.
